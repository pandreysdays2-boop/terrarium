What is this?

Plain English guide to everything happening in The Terrarium.

Bloom

Named after Leopold Bloom, the wandering protagonist of James Joyce's Ulysses. Some names just fit.

What is val loss?

Val loss (validation loss) is how we measure how well Bloom has learned language. It's a number — lower is better.

During training, we hold back a small slice of text that Bloom never sees. Every few hundred steps, we ask her to predict that text. How wrong she is on average is the val loss.

Think of it like a vocabulary test. 4.5 = guessing randomly. 3.3 = she's genuinely learned something about how language works.

Train loss is how wrong she is on text she has seen — always lower. The gap between them shows if she's memorizing vs. actually learning.

What are the stages?

Bloom trains in two stages, like a school curriculum.

Stage 1 · Pretrain — Bloom reads a wide library of 150 books (138MB of text). This teaches her general language: grammar, sentence structure, vocabulary, narrative flow. 15,000 training steps.

Stage 2 · Mid — Bloom graduates to a more curated 115-book collection (158MB), the best literary fiction. She refines everything she learned. 60,000 steps.

Stage 3 (fine-tuning on a tiny "best of the best" corpus) was attempted but dropped — the fine corpus was too small and caused overfitting.

What is overfitting?

Overfitting is when Bloom starts memorising instead of learning.

If her training loss keeps dropping but her val loss stops improving (or gets worse), she's memorised the training texts but can't generalize to new language. Like a student who memorised the practice exam but can't answer the real one.

The overfit gap chart shows val − train. Anything above 0.3 is a warning sign. Bloom currently sits around 0.09 — healthy.

What is a GPT?

GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) is the architecture behind ChatGPT, Claude, and most modern language models.

Bloom is a tiny version — 63.6 million parameters (weights, or numbers she can adjust). GPT-2 has 117M, GPT-3 has 175 billion. Bloom is small but entirely built and trained from scratch.

She has 12 layers of self-attention, which lets her understand how words relate to each other across a 128-token context window.

Frequently asked questions

  • Why is this public? Most AI companies don't show their training curves — competition, hype management, ugly process. Bloom has none of those problems. The slow burn is the content.
  • How long does a full run take? About 75,000 steps total, spread across multiple sessions. The GPU runs hot so there's a deliberate step delay between training steps to keep things stable.
  • What hardware is Bloom training on? A consumer gaming laptop with an RTX 5060 (8GB VRAM). Not a data center — a laptop on a desk somewhere.
  • When is Bloom done? The target val loss is 3.3. She's currently at 3.4735. Each run gets her closer. She's done when she gets there, or when we discover she can't.
  • What will Bloom be used for? Who knows. She might just be a beautiful thing that learned to write like the Victorian novel section of a library. We'll see when she gets there.
